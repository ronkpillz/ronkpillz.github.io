Business Problem

Following a strategic business pivot in Q4 2025, SALIENT Ventures experienced a significant increase in transaction volume. However, management lacked sufficient visibility into whether this growth represented sustainable business performance or simply higher sales activity. The objective of this analysis was to evaluate revenue, profitability, customer behaviour, and product performance to support informed budgeting and strategic planning for 2026.

Key Insights

  • Total revenue for Q4 2025 reached $114.74M, generating a total profit of $17.21M with a profit margin of 15%.
  • Texas emerged as the most profitable city, generating approximately $6.10M in profit, significantly outperforming all other cities.
  • Accessories was the highest-performing product category with approximately $4M in profit, followed by Games and Gym products.
  • Gold Bracelets was both the most profitable product and the best-selling product by quantity, selling 480 units.
  • Female customers generated approximately 58% of total revenue, while male customers contributed 42%.
  • The Youth and Early Youth age groups generated the highest revenue, making them the company's most valuable customer segments.
  • Revenue remained consistently higher than profit throughout Weeks 40–53, with noticeable peaks around Weeks 47 and 51, indicating periods of stronger sales performance.

Business Recommendations

  • Increase marketing and operational investment in Texas to sustain its strong profitability while investigating why other cities are underperforming.
  • Expand the Accessories product category and ensure adequate inventory since it delivers the highest profit among all categories.
  • Promote Gold Bracelets through targeted campaigns because it performs exceptionally well in both profitability and sales volume.
  • Develop personalised marketing campaigns targeting female customers, who currently contribute the majority of total revenue.
  • Launch customer engagement initiatives aimed at the Youth and Early Youth segments to strengthen customer loyalty and maximise lifetime value.
  • Review sales activities during lower-performing weeks to identify factors affecting revenue and implement strategies that replicate the success achieved during peak weeks.
  • Continue monitoring profitability alongside revenue to ensure future sales growth translates into sustainable business performance.
